# AI Playbook

## Purpose
Use AI safely and usefully at Fawazlaw.sa as a **support tool**, never as a substitute for licensed legal advice. AI must respect Saudi Bar regulations, PDPL, and the firm's confidentiality obligations.

## Standards
- Every AI-touched surface displays the disclaimer: AI is a support tool, not a replacement for licensed legal advice.
- No client-identifying data is sent to a model provider without explicit, logged consent.
- All client-facing AI output destined for publication or formal communication is reviewed by a licensed practitioner.
- All AI calls are rate-limited per user and per IP.
- All AI prompts/responses involving client matters are auditable (with PII redaction).
- Model + version + temperature + system prompt are recorded for every AI call.

## Checklist
- [ ] Disclaimer visible above the input on every AI surface.
- [ ] PII redaction applied before sending to the provider.
- [ ] Output reviewed by a practitioner before any client-facing use.
- [ ] Per-user and per-IP rate limit in place.
- [ ] Logged: model, version, prompt hash, response hash, reviewer.
- [ ] No reliance on AI for jurisdictional or regulatory citations without practitioner verification.

## Best Practices
- Prefer **RAG over a curated, practitioner-approved Saudi legal corpus** to bare LLM generation.
- Use **structured outputs** (JSON schemas / tool calls) for parts of the response that drive UI or downstream logic.
- Keep **system prompts versioned** in code; treat them as source artefacts.
- Use **lower temperatures (≤ 0.3)** for anything claim-bearing; higher only for clearly creative tasks (subject lines, suggestions).
- **Refuse rather than guess** — design prompts and post-filters to say "I'm not sure; please consult a licensed lawyer at Fawazlaw" when confidence is low.
- Maintain a **red-team prompt set** (jailbreaks, PII probes, adversarial questions about disallowed advice) and run it before any AI release.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Treating fluent output as accurate output.
- Sending client documents to model providers without redaction and consent.
- Citing legal articles or court precedents generated by the model without practitioner verification.
- Hiding the disclaimer behind a click or a long page scroll.
- Using AI for billing decisions, fee quotes, or anything with direct financial impact without a human gate.
- Saving model prompts/responses with raw PII in long-term storage.

## Project-Specific Implementation Guidance
- Public-facing assistant: scope strictly to general information; route any specific-matter question to a human practitioner.
- Internal practitioner tools: lower friction allowed (no disclaimer in internal-only views) but redaction + audit log still apply.
- AI-generated articles are explicitly forbidden from publication without practitioner authorship and review.
- Provider choice: pick the provider with the strongest enterprise data-handling commitments; document the choice and DPA in `docs/compliance/ai-providers.md` (planned).

## Recommended Tools
- OpenAI / Anthropic enterprise tiers with zero-retention options.
- Vector DBs: pgvector, Qdrant, or Weaviate for RAG.
- Evaluation: `promptfoo`, `ragas`, or hand-rolled goldsets in `tests/ai/`.
- Guardrails / structured outputs: provider-native JSON mode, `instructor`, or `outlines`.

## Validation Steps
- Run the red-team prompt set; record pass/fail.
- Spot-check 10 random AI outputs for hallucination and PII leakage before each release.
- Verify the disclaimer renders in the production build.

## Definition of Done
- All checklist items pass.
- Practitioner sign-off recorded for any new AI-driven user surface.
- `SECURITY.md` and `docs/compliance/` updated for any new data flow.
