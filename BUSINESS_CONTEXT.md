# Business Context

## Fawazlaw.sa Business Purpose
Fawazlaw.sa is the digital home of **شركة فواز للمحاماة والاستشارات القانونية** — a Saudi law firm offering legal representation, advisory, and dispute resolution services to individuals and companies. The platform's purpose is to (1) build trust and authority in the Saudi legal market, (2) convert qualified leads into consultations, and (3) provide a digital client experience that scales the firm's practice.

## Legal Services Context
Practice areas reflected in the site include (but are not limited to):
- Commercial and corporate law
- Labour and employment law
- Family and personal status law
- Real estate and property law
- Criminal defence
- Arbitration and dispute resolution
- Contracts and legal advisory
- Corporate governance and compliance
- Intellectual property
- Litigation and representation before Saudi courts and tribunals

Each service surface must be written and reviewed by a licensed practitioner. The website never substitutes for licensed legal advice.

## Saudi Market Context
- Saudi Vision 2030 and Najiz court digitisation are accelerating LegalTech adoption.
- Arabic-first communication is mandatory; English is required for enterprise and expat segments.
- Trust signals matter heavily: licensed lawyers, named partners, verifiable credentials, professional design, and confidentiality assurances.
- Mobile-first is a hard requirement — most discovery traffic is mobile.
- Local payment methods (mada, Apple Pay, STC Pay) via MyFatoorah or equivalent are expected.

## GCC Expansion Potential
- UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, Oman share Arabic-first expectations and similar dispute/contract patterns.
- Cross-border commercial work (KSA ⇄ UAE in particular) is a near-term growth lane.
- GCC expansion requires localisation of content, jurisdiction labels, and payment/billing.

## Differentiation
- Saudi-first content depth — written by practitioners, for the Saudi context.
- Digital-native client experience (planned client portal, document workflows, AI-assisted support).
- Premium brand presentation that matches the trust expectation of legal services.
- Transparent positioning of AI as a support tool, not a substitute for licensed advice.

## Revenue Opportunities
1. **Consultations** (hourly / fixed-fee) — primary near-term conversion.
2. **Retainers** for SMEs and enterprises.
3. **Productised legal packages** (incorporation, employment kit, contract review) — high-margin, repeatable.
4. **Subscription client portal** with document management and concierge legal help.
5. **AI-assisted self-serve** (with clear human escalation) for low-complexity matters.

## Strategic Opportunities
- Become the most authoritative Arabic legal content destination for Saudi search intent.
- Partner with HR/finance SaaS platforms for embedded legal services.
- Win enterprise procurement via PDPL-compliant client portal and audit logs.
- Establish thought leadership via published case studies (anonymised) and regulatory commentary.

## Risks and Assumptions
- **Regulatory** — Bar regulations and PDPL set firm constraints on marketing, fee structures, and data handling.
- **Reputational** — incorrect legal information published online can damage the firm's standing; all public legal content requires practitioner sign-off.
- **Operational** — over-reliance on AI without human review is unacceptable.
- **Technical** — current dependency on CRA and a dual API stack creates maintenance overhead; plan migration.
- **Assumption** — Arabic-first remains correct for the foreseeable future; English is a strategic secondary, not equal.
