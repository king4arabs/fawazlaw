# Marketing & Growth

## SEO Strategy
- **Intent mapping:** every primary practice area maps to one canonical Arabic landing page with a clear H1, structured outline, FAQ block, and CTA.
- **Local SEO:** target Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam, Mecca, Madinah with city-qualified pages where the firm has presence; add `LocalBusiness` schema and verified Google Business Profile.
- **Schema:** `Organization`, `LegalService`, `LocalBusiness`, `Article`, `BreadcrumbList`, `FAQPage`, `Person` (for named partners) — all validated with Google's Rich Results Test.
- **Technical SEO:** canonical URLs, valid `sitemap.xml`, `robots.txt`, descriptive `<title>` (50–60 chars) and meta description (140–160 chars), Open Graph + Twitter cards, hreflang once English is fully translated.
- **Performance SEO:** Lighthouse mobile performance ≥ 90 target; LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, INP < 200ms.
- **Internal linking:** every service page links to ≥ 3 supporting articles; every article links back to ≥ 1 service page.

## Content Strategy
- Editorial calendar planned by Compliance Advisor + Marketing.
- Article taxonomy: practice area → topic cluster → specific question.
- Every article is reviewed by a practitioner before publication; mark reviewer + review date in metadata.
- Maintain a glossary of Saudi legal terminology (Arabic + English) for consistency.
- Update key articles annually; older articles get a "last reviewed" date.

## Arabic / English Growth Strategy
- **Arabic (primary):** treat as the default language; do not ship machine-translated content.
- **English (secondary):** for enterprise, expat, and GCC cross-border audiences; ship only when human-translated and reviewed.
- Use the `lang` and `dir` attributes correctly; never mix translations inside one document.
- Future: `hreflang="ar-SA"` and `hreflang="en-SA"` once English coverage is complete.

## Legal Service Landing Pages
Each landing page must contain:
1. H1 with the service name in Arabic (and English where translated).
2. Trust intro (one short paragraph).
3. Who it's for (bullet list).
4. What's included (bullet list).
5. Process steps (3–5 steps).
6. FAQ (≥ 5 questions with structured data).
7. Primary CTA (consultation / WhatsApp / contact form).
8. Internal links to related services and articles.
9. Practitioner attribution and last reviewed date.

## Conversion Optimization
- One primary CTA per page; avoid stacking competing CTAs.
- Mobile sticky call/WhatsApp button on service pages.
- Above-the-fold trust signals (years of practice, named partners, languages spoken).
- Reduce form friction: 3 fields max on first contact (name, phone, message).
- A/B test CTA wording quarterly; record results in `DECISIONS.md`.

## Lead Generation
- Primary channels: organic search (SEO), Google Business Profile, direct.
- Secondary channels: paid search (Arabic queries), LinkedIn (enterprise), referrals.
- Capture: contact form, WhatsApp, phone — all funnelled into a single CRM (planned).
- Qualify leads: matter type, urgency, jurisdiction, budget signal.

## Analytics Setup
- GA4 (or Plausible) for traffic, source, page performance, and conversion events.
- Define conversion events: `contact_submit`, `whatsapp_click`, `phone_click`, `app_portal_click`, `service_page_engaged`.
- Search Console for query, CTR, position monitoring on Arabic and English.
- Optional: PostHog or Mixpanel for product analytics in the client portal.

## Social Proof
- Anonymised case studies (Arabic + English).
- Verifiable practitioner profiles with credentials.
- Client testimonials with explicit written consent.
- Press, awards, and partnerships **only when verifiable** — never invent.

## Authority-Building Content
- Regulatory commentary on PDPL, Companies Law, Labour Law amendments.
- Plain-language explainers ("How to … in Saudi Arabia").
- Webinars and Q&A in Arabic for SMEs.
- Annual practice-area outlook reports.

## Saudi Legal Market Positioning
- "Premium, practitioner-led, digitally native Saudi law firm."
- Bilingual capability (Arabic / English) with enterprise-ready service.
- AI-assisted service that always preserves human professional accountability.
- PDPL-aware, audit-ready operations — a differentiator for enterprise buyers.
